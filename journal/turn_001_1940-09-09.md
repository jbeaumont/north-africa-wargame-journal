# Journal Entry — Turn 1, 09 September 1940

---

So it begins. The war in the Western Desert — all ten feet of map, all 1,600 counters, all God-knows-how-many cubic centimeters of imaginary water — has commenced. I've spent the better part of three hours just setting up the initial positions and working through the reinforcement schedule, and already the logistics are eating me alive.

Let me start with what should be the good news. Graziani's 10th Army HQ has arrived at hex 1405, and with it the 63rd Infantry Division *Cirene* and the 62nd *Marmarica* are filtering onto the map in adjacent hexes, stretching from 1404 to 1506. Fresh divisions, theoretically ready to begin the grand march eastward toward Sidi Barrani and, eventually, the Suez Canal. Mussolini's grand vision. The reality, of course, is somewhat different.

The reality is that six Italian infantry regiments — the 125th, 126th, 115th, 116th, 141st, and 142nd — are already critically short of water. They have just *arrived* and they are already degraded. The Libyan colonial regiments are even worse: the 1st through 4th are fuel-critical, and the 2nd Libyan Division HQ, 3rd Libyan Regiment, 141st Catanzaro, and the 4th Blackshirt Division are fully out of supply. On **Turn One**. I haven't moved a single unit east.

And the pasta. Oh, the pasta. Six regiments — essentially the entire combat strength of *Cirene*, *Marmarica*, and *Catanzaro* divisions — are pasta-deprived this week. I know it sounds absurd. I know that in any other wargame, "pasta allocation" would be a joke. But in CNA, it is deadly serious: Italian morale modifiers for combat are already dire, and without their rations these men will fight like men who haven't eaten properly. Which they haven't. I am running an army whose principal strategic challenge in Week One is not the enemy but the caloric requirements of Mediterranean infantry.

Meanwhile, Tobruk depot is running low, and I've evaporated — *evaporated* — 20.3 fuel points to the Libyan sun. That fuel simply ceased to exist because I stored it in the desert in September. I could scream.

Now, wearing my British hat: the situation looks manageable but fragile. Only seven Allied units on the map, and the Desert Rats are in rough shape. 7th Armoured Brigade is down to one-third steps, 4th Armoured Brigade at two-thirds, the divisional HQ at half. These are my only mobile striking force, and they're already damaged — presumably from the border skirmishing that the scenario assumes has been ongoing. The 4th Indian Division is intact, at least, with 5th and 11th Brigades ready, but they're infantry. Everyone is water-critical. *Everyone.* British, Indian, Italian, Libyan — the entire theatre is parched.

The strategic calculus is stark. As Graziani, I need to push east, but I can barely sustain the forces I have in place. As Wavell, I need to preserve my battered armour and wait — Compass is months away, and I cannot afford to lose the 7th Armoured Division in some pointless encounter when my real advantage lies in patience and the eventual buildup of forces that the Italians simply cannot match.

My decision this turn: no major offensive movement for either side. The Italians will reorganize, try to establish a functioning supply chain from Tobruk forward, and pray that next week's logistics phase is kinder. The British will screen the frontier and husband their strength.

I have ninety-nine turns to go. I have already spent more time calculating water evaporation rates than I spent on my taxes last year.

God help us all.
