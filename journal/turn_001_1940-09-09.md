## Campaign for North Africa — Turn 1 Session Notes (09 September 1940)

Opening turn. Phil's Italians arrive on the map in force — or at least in numbers. The 10th Army HQ sets up at 1405 along with 63rd "Cirene" and 62nd "Marmarica" divisions deploying across 1404–1506. Catanzaro (64th) and both Libyan divisions were already in place. The Maletti Group starts at two-thirds strength, which is historically correct and immediately annoying for Phil.

The supply picture is where Turn 1 gets educational. Nearly every unit on both sides is water-critical. This is the Western Desert doing what it does — the water logistics network barely exists yet, and the consumption rates are punishing. More consequentially, six Italian infantry regiments (both Cirene regiments, both Marmarica, both Catanzaro) are pasta-deprived, triggering the morale penalty for Italian units not receiving their pasta ration. Berg's infamous rule, working exactly as written. Phil noted that his fresh reinforcements arrived already hungry, which tells you something about the state of the Tobruk depot.

Fuel situation is arguably worse. The four Libyan infantry regiments are fuel-critical, and 20.3 fuel points evaporated this turn — a fifth of nothing, but it compounds. Three units (141st Catanzaro, 2nd Libyan Division HQ, 3rd Libyan Regiment) are fully out of supply. Tobruk depot is flagged low.

Terry's Allies are comparatively comfortable: 7th Armoured and 4th Indian fully supplied, no shortages. Eight units, zero problems.

Heading into Turn 2, Phil needs to sort the supply chain before anything offensive is thinkable. Anthony confirmed the evaporation calculation twice.
