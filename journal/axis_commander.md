## Campaign Lessons

### Turn 7 — 21 October 1940

Seven turns of identical failure, now terminal. The 125th Cirene at -19 cohesion confirms what §38.5 makes mathematically inevitable: pasta deprivation compounds cohesion loss each Operations Stage, and once a unit passes roughly -12, recovery requires more supply than the unit is worth. I let three regiments starve for seven turns because I kept writing "move them into trace" without verifying the MP cost of the actual hex path to within 5 of Alpha at 1405. The lesson is that triage decisions made on Turn 5 should have been made on Turn 2. Every turn of partial supply to doomed units was supply stolen from viable ones.

Fuel evaporation at 18.2 points is tracking lower as dump levels decrease — Alpha at 69/80, Beta at 52/60. The 3% tax is self-correcting downward as stocks deplete, which is a grim kind of efficiency. Benghazi at 120/200 represents 80 points of unused capacity that I must fill next convoy cycle. The surviving force that greets Rommel at Turn 14 depends entirely on whether I stop sentimentalizing dead divisions and start concentrating supply on what lives.

### Turn 5 — 07 October 1940

Five turns of the same failure. The out-of-supply units — 125th Cirene, 62nd Marmarica HQ, 115th and 116th Marmarica — have been outside trace of Alpha at 1405 since approximately Turn 1. I said "move them toward 1405" every single turn. They're still out. Either I'm not executing movement orders or their movement allowance can't close the distance given terrain costs on the coastal escarpment. I need to verify the actual hex path and MP cost from their current positions to within 5 hexes of 1405. If it's physically impossible in one turn, I should have been incrementally closing since Turn 1 and clearly wasn't.

Fuel evaporation at 19.1 cumulative is slightly below last turn's 19.9 — probably rounding from reduced dump levels. Alpha at 72/80, Beta at 54/60, Benghazi at 120/200. The lesson is brutal and simple: units I cannot supply, I must abandon. Triage is the only remaining logistics strategy.

### Turn 3 — 23 September 1940

The pasta checklist I swore I'd implement on Turn 1 has now failed three turns running. 125th Cirene, 126th Cirene, and 115th Marmarica remain deprived. I need to confront the mechanical reality: these units are probably out of supply trace, meaning pasta can't reach them regardless of allocation intent. Section 38.5 requires supply trace for ration delivery. If 125th and 126th can't trace to a depot, no checklist fixes starvation. The root cause isn't bookkeeping — it's positional. These regiments must physically move into trace range of Alpha at 1405 or Benghazi at 1201 before pasta allocation matters.

Fuel evaporation hit 19.9 points cumulative. Alpha dropped from 77 to 75, Beta from 58 to 56. The 3% Italian tax is steady and predictable. Benghazi at 120/200 has capacity headroom — I should route replenishment there rather than topping off Tripoli at 988/999 where marginal supply just evaporates into storage losses. Every point forward is a point that might reach a unit's mouth.

### Turn 1 — 09 September 1940

Three failures, all self-inflicted, all logistical. The 20.3 fuel points lost to evaporation across all depots reinforces one principle: never stockpile fuel in forward dumps beyond what I'll consume within two turns. Alpha and Beta should be flow-through points, not reservoirs. Every additional dump is another 3% Italian tax compounding independently.

The out-of-supply gap between Benghazi at 1201 and Alpha at 1405 caught three units — the 141st Catanzaro and both Libyan formations. Root cause: I positioned units assuming proximity to the road axis meant proximity to supply. It doesn't. The 5-hex trace is rigid and terrain-dependent. I need to physically verify trace distance during deployment, not assume it.

The pasta failure on the three arriving regiments is pure bookkeeping negligence. Section 38.5 requires explicit allocation; arrivals don't inherit it. I'm institutionalizing a pre-movement pasta check — every Italian infantry unit, every Administrative Phase, no exceptions. Cohesion damage from hunger is the most absurd way to lose combat power in this game, and I nearly normalized it on Turn 1.

### Turn 2 — 16 September 1940

The pasta failure recurring is inexcusable. I said I'd institutionalize a pre-movement check. I didn't execute it. Three units still deprived means I either failed to allocate during the Administrative Phase or allocated incorrectly — either way, a process failure, not a knowledge gap. Starting now: I write down every Italian infantry regiment by name and check off pasta allocation before touching anything else. Physical checklist, pen on paper.

The water crisis across six units reveals a second systemic problem. I focused Turn 1 on fuel evaporation and supply trace distance but neglected water distribution as a separate logistical layer. Water isn't just supply status — it's tracked per Operations Stage and requires deliberate allocation from dumps. Alpha at 1405 dropped to 77/80, Beta at 1302 to 58/60. Those dumps are sustaining units near them but units between or beyond the dumps are dying of thirst. I need to consolidate formations around existing water sources, not spread them across the road axis hoping proximity suffices.

### Turn 4 — 30 September 1940

The consolidation order I issued last turn didn't execute. Four units remain out of supply, three units remain pasta-deprived, and now three regiments have crossed into disorganized status. The root cause is no longer positional ambiguity — it's that I'm issuing intentions without verifying execution. I said "consolidate onto the 1302-1405 axis." I didn't confirm movement orders for specific units to specific hexes.

New protocol: assign each out-of-supply unit a destination hex within 4 hexes of Alpha at 1405. The 141st and 142nd Catanzaro move first — they're still recoverable. The three disorganized regiments (125th Cirene, 126th Cirene, 115th Marmarica) get parked adjacent to Beta at 1302 for water and pasta recovery, accepting they won't fight for weeks.

Fuel evaporation totals 19.6 cumulative. Alpha at 74/80, Beta at 55/60 — steady drain. Benghazi at 120/200 remains underutilized. I'm routing next replenishment convoy there, not Tripoli.

### Turn 6 — 14 October 1940

The 115th Marmarica hitting -18 cohesion from pasta deprivation confirms what I suspected at Turn 4: once an Italian regiment crosses roughly -12 cohesion from hunger, recovery is mathematically impossible within the campaign timeline. The §38.5 penalty compounds faster than rest and supply can restore. I kept feeding these units supply hoping to stabilize them. That was wrong. The triage threshold is -10 cohesion — below that, cut them off and redirect resources.

Cumulative fuel evaporation is now 18.6 points reported this turn alone; Alpha at 71/80 and Beta at 53/60 are bleeding steadily. Beta has lost 7 points over the campaign, roughly confirming the 3% Italian rate compounding. I should draw Beta down to operational minimum and push excess fuel forward to Alpha, reducing the evaporation base at Beta rather than maintaining two half-full dumps.

The Australians at 2013-2014 are the real threat now. Fresh divisions change the calculus by Turn 9-10. My screening line must hold around 1405 with functional units only.
