## Campaign for North Africa — Turn 1 (09 September 1940)

The campaign opens with Graziani's 10th Army arriving at the Libyan-Egyptian frontier in roughly historical fashion. Phil's Italian force enters the map concentrated around hexes 1404–1506, with 63rd 'Cirene' and 62nd 'Marmarica' divisions deploying in a tight cluster. Twenty active Axis units against Terry's seven — on paper an overwhelming numerical advantage, but the supply picture immediately complicates things.

The logistics situation is, bluntly, a mess. Nearly everything on the board is water-critical. This includes all of Terry's units, so both sides are feeling the desert equally in that respect. More damaging for Phil: six Italian infantry regiments — both regiments each from Cirene, Marmarica, and Catanzaro — are pasta-deprived, triggering the Italian morale/effectiveness penalties. Turn 1 and already the pasta rule is doing work. The Tobruk depot is flagged as low, which explains the cascading shortages forward.

Three Axis units (141st 'Catanzaro', 2nd Libyan Division HQ, and 3rd Libyan Regiment) are fully out of supply. The four Libyan regiments are also fuel-critical, limiting their ability to reposition. Phil lost 20.3 fuel points to evaporation — a number that will only climb as summer heat lingers.

On the Allied side, Terry's position is tidy. 4th Armoured Brigade has taken two steps of damage — unclear whether from the deployment scenario or an error we need to check — but otherwise 7th Armoured and 4th Indian are intact and in supply.

No combat this turn. Next week Phil needs to sort out Tobruk's depot capacity before attempting any forward movement, or those out-of-supply units will start degrading fast.

10th Army HQ at 1405 gives me a command structure but no miracles. Thirteen turns until the DAK shows up with competent supply management.

**Terry (Allied):** Right, so we're on the map. 7th Armoured Division HQ at 2010, 4th Armoured Brigade at 2009, 7th Armoured Brigade at 2011, 4th RHA co-located with the HQ at 2010. Nice tight deployment sitting just south of the coastal corridor with the Qattara Depression anchoring everything to the south. Seven active units total, nobody out of supply — though the supply snapshot says 3 units OOS, which contradicts my active roster, so I need to check whether that's counting garrison detachments I'm not tracking properly.

The 20.3 points of fuel evaporation stings more than I expected for Turn 1. That's our 7% rate already biting. I need to be disciplined about not stockpiling fuel forward until I actually need it.

Phil's 10th Army is sitting there looking large and doing nothing, which is historically appropriate — Graziani took until 13 September to cross the wire. I'm content to mirror that passivity. No reason to push west. Let his pasta spoil and his fuel evaporate at his worse rate. DAK is thirteen turns away, and every turn Phil's logistics degrade is a turn I'm winning without fighting.

Next turn I want to confirm supply line integrity back to Alexandria and start thinking about where 7th Armoured's forward screen should sit.

## Gamemaster's Ruling

Eleven checks run this turn covering the usual spread — impassable hex occupation, step counts, fuel and water bounds, map positions, elimination marking, disorganization thresholds, hex control, morale, depot capacity, and the reinforcement schedule. Everything came back clean. The 10th Army HQ and elements of the 63rd "Cirene" and 62nd "Marmarica" arriving at hexes 1404–1506 all match the §4.1 reinforcement timetable for Week 1, so no issues there.

Two things worth flagging, though neither is a violation yet. First, the 141st Infantry Regiment "Catanzaro," the 2nd Libyan Division HQ, and the 3rd Libyan Infantry Regiment are all out of supply. If this persists into Turn 2, §13.3 attrition applies — the Italian player needs to sort the supply trace or accept step losses. Second, the three pasta-deprived regiments (125th, 126th, 115th) are accumulating morale drag per §13.2.3; nothing breaks this turn, but it compounds. The 20.3 points of fuel evaporation is within expected norms for September heat per §13.1.2.

Verdict: turn stands as played. No corrections required.
