18 November 1941

The offensive opened today under clear skies — weather normal across the front, which was all we could have asked for at this time of year. Eighth Army held the initiative, and Corps HQ pushed forward with the intention of exploiting what intelligence suggested was a deeply compromised Axis logistical situation. The reports proved accurate beyond expectation: every Axis formation we can account for — all thirty-four — is now assessed as out of supply. Rommel's forces are, for the moment, living on what they have in hand. If we can maintain pressure before resupply reaches them, the opportunity is considerable.

That said, our own supply picture is far from comfortable. Fourteen of our thirty-six formations are operating beyond reliable lines of communication, including 4th Indian Division, 1st South African Division, the Polish Brigade, 11th Infantry Brigade, and 7th Support Group. These are not marginal units; they represent a substantial portion of our fighting strength, and their commanders have made clear that ammunition and fuel stocks will constrain operations if the situation is not remedied within days.

The most troubling development concerns 7th Armoured Division's headquarters, which slipped from out-of-supply status to critical during the first operational stage. Without a functioning divisional HQ able to coordinate its brigades, the armoured thrust risks losing coherence at precisely the moment we need it most. Efforts to re-establish supply links were unsuccessful through the day. Similarly, 7th Infantry Brigade deteriorated rapidly — entering the turn in supply, falling out during the first stage, and reaching critical status by the second. The speed of that collapse suggests the brigade advanced beyond its logistic tether faster than the supply echelons could follow. Someone at division will need to answer for that planning.

On a more encouraging note, 64th Medium Artillery Regiment, which had briefly lost its supply connection, was restored to full status by the third operational stage. The gunners will be needed; we cannot afford to have medium artillery silent during the opening days of a corps-level offensive.

No major engagements were recorded. Both sides appear to be manoeuvring, with our forces pressing forward and the Axis, presumably, scrambling to restore supply routes before we can bring them to battle under disadvantageous conditions.

The situation at day's end is cautiously promising: the enemy's universal supply crisis presents a window of opportunity, but our own overextension — particularly around 7th Armoured's headquarters — threatens to close it before we can act decisively.
