# Pre-Campaign Setup Inspection

*Conducted before Turn 1 — 09 September 1940*

---

I have conducted the official pre-game board inspection for our Operation E campaign, commencing with the September 1940 scenario, and I am pleased to report my findings in full.

Regarding the map verification, I can confirm under §MAP-1 that the correct Campaign for North Africa map sheet is in use, with one hundred and thirty-three hexes tallied, falling well within the expected range of one hundred fifteen to one hundred fifty-five. Under §MAP-2 I have verified the geographic span from column 04 through column 22, encompassing the theatre from Morocco to the Nile Delta. I personally traced the eight key strategic positions required under §MAP-3 — Tripoli, Benghazi, Tobruk, Mersa Matruh, El Alamein, Alexandria, Cairo, and the Qattara Depression — and all are present and correctly situated. I was particularly attentive to §MAP-4, confirming that the Qattara Depression at hex 1910 is marked impassable with no exploitable adjacencies, thereby anchoring the southern flank as intended.

Turning to deployment, all checks from §SET-1 through §SET-8 have passed without exception. The Italian 10th Army, including the 63rd Infantry Division "Cirene" and its constituent regiments, is correctly marshalled in Libya west of column 17, while the Western Desert Force, led by the renowned 7th Armoured Division — the Desert Rats — with the 4th and 7th Armoured Brigades and 4th Royal Horse Artillery, holds Egypt east of column 18. The force ratio of twenty Axis units against eight Allied stands at 2.5 to 1, historically plausible under §SET-5. Both the Tripoli Base Supply Depot at hex 1001 and the Cairo Base Supply Depot at hex 2201 are correctly positioned per §SET-6, and all supply counters rest on passable terrain per §SET-7. Fuel sources are confirmed accessible on both sides under §SET-8.

I note no warnings, no irregularities, and no discrepancies of any kind across all twelve checks. The board is correctly set. Commence operations.
