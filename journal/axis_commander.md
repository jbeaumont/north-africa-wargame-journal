## Current Strategic Assessment

*Updated Turn 1 — 09 September 1940*

The campaign opens with significant logistical dysfunction that must be resolved before any eastward advance can be contemplated. Of 19 active units, three are out of supply entirely — 141st Infantry Regiment 'Catanzaro', 2nd Libyan Division HQ, and 3rd Libyan Infantry Regiment — and three newly arrived regiments (125th, 126th 'Cirene' and 115th 'Marmarica') are already pasta-deprived, suffering cohesion degradation before they have fired a shot. This is unacceptable.

Fuel evaporation consumed 20.3 points on Turn 1 alone. With Fuel Dump Alpha at 78/80 (hex 1405) and Beta at 59/60 (hex 1302), we cannot afford profligate movement. Benghazi depot sits at only 120/200 — dangerously lean for sustaining the advance formations.

The British have deployed 7th Armoured Division around hex 2010 and 4th Indian Division near 2015 — a mobile screen with infantry backstop. They are outnumbered but better supplied.

Immediate priority: consolidate supply lines to all forward units, resolve the pasta shortage for arriving divisions, and establish a coherent forward depot structure before attempting the advance toward Sidi Barrani.

## Campaign Lessons

### Turn 1 — 09 September 1940

Three critical findings from the opening turn. First, the 5-hex supply radius is an iron constraint. The out-of-supply status of 141st 'Catanzaro', 2nd Libyan HQ, and 3rd Libyan Regiment reveals they are positioned beyond effective depot range. Their exact hexes must be identified and either the units pulled back or a forward supply point established immediately. Every turn out of supply compounds degradation.

Second, pasta logistics for arriving formations failed completely. 125th, 126th 'Cirene' and 115th 'Marmarica' arrived at hexes 1404-1506 with no pasta allocation. The arrival schedule was known; this was a planning failure at the intendenza level. Henceforth, pasta stocks must be pre-positioned at arrival hexes one turn prior.

Third, fuel evaporation of 20.3 points — roughly 3% of total Axis fuel holdings — confirms that fuel is a wasting asset. Every point spent on unnecessary movement is a point lost twice: once to consumption, once to evaporation of the remainder. Movement discipline is not optional; it is survival.
